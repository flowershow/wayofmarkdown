## Pick your entrance

The map has two natural entrances, and both walk the same spine:

- 🚀 **"I want to publish something"** — website, blog, a page on the web. Start at ①; something live by ② — today.
- 📝 **"I want better notes"** — knowledge base, second brain, maybe published later or never. Start at ①, skim ②, and the publishing steps will be waiting when you care.

## The spine, in words

- **⓪ Why bother?** *(optional, 5 min)* — [why markdown-based](why.md), or the punchier [manifesto](manifesto.md). Skip if convinced.
- **① Learn markdown** *(10–20 min)* — [Markdown Basics](basics.md); the syntax is genuinely small. Side quests: [markdown everywhere](everywhere.md), [markdown + AI](markdown-in-chatgpt.md), [pluggable markdown](pluggable-markdown.md).
- **② Publish your first page** *(20 min)* — the "GitHub direct method", nothing installed: [tutorial 1](learn/tutorials/create-a-website-from-scratch.md). Or the smallest version: [publish a single document](publish-markdown.md).
- **③ Go local: Obsidian + GitHub Desktop** *(40 min)* — [tutorial 2](learn/tutorials/edit-a-website-locally.md), plus [what makes Obsidian special](markdown-in-obsidian.md).
- **④ Structure your stuff** *(30 min)* — [build a catalog of anything](learn/howtos/create-a-simple-catalog-of-anything.md), then [the markdown database pattern](markdown-database.md) behind it.
- **⑤ Work with others** *(20 min)* — [tutorial 3](learn/tutorial-3.md), [GitHub tips](kb/github-tips.md), the [glossary](kb/glossary.md) and the [git rescue guide](kb/git-rescue.md) for when things go sideways.
- **⑥ Build your X** — [the full guide index](markdown-based.md): [website](markdown-websites.md) · [blog](markdown-blogs.md) · [knowledge base](markdown-knowledge-bases.md) · [Notion replacement](markdown-notion-alternative.md) · [database](markdown-databases.md) · [wiki](markdown-wikis.md) · [garden](markdown-digital-gardens.md) · [docs](markdown-docs.md) · [journal](markdown-journals.md) · [bookmarks](markdown-bookmarking.md) · [book](markdown-books.md)
