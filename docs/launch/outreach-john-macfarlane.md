# John MacFarlane — pandoc author, CommonMark co-architect

**Why**: the quiet giant. pandoc is the universal converter underpinning half the ecosystem; CommonMark is why "markdown" means one thing. Philosophy professor (Berkeley) — thoughtful long-form answers likely. Reach: email (address on his Berkeley/johnmacfarlane.net page).

## Email draft

Subject: Short written interview about pandoc and markdown's quiet takeover

Dear John,

I run wayofmarkdown.com, a site making the case for markdown-based working — websites, knowledge bases, books — for a general audience. Practically everything the site recommends stands on your work at one layer or another, which most users never realise.

I'm doing a short written-interview series with the people who built this ecosystem. Would you be willing to answer five questions over email? You'd see and could edit the final piece before publication.

With admiration for pandoc, which I have used near-daily for a decade,

Rufus Pollock

## Draft questions

1. pandoc turns 20 soon and quietly powers an enormous amount of publishing. What did you originally build it for, and when did you realise it had escaped?
2. CommonMark standardised a format Gruber famously declined to. Looking back, what did standardisation get right and what did it foreclose?
3. Markdown keeps absorbing use cases people said needed richer formats — books, theses, docs. Where do you think its actual limit is?
4. LLMs speak markdown natively, arguably because of the corpus your tools helped create. Does the AI era change what a document format should be?
5. What should a markdown-curious person understand about document formats that they probably don't?
