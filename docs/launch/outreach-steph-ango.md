**Why him first**: his essay "File over app" (stephango.com/file-over-app) *is* our thesis from the tool-builder's side. Obsidian's community is exactly our audience. Active on X (@kepano); known to answer thoughtful cold emails/DMs.

## Draft questions

1. "File over app" has become a rallying cry well beyond Obsidian. Did you expect that, and has its meaning drifted from what you intended?
2. Obsidian could have used a proprietary format and probably locked in users harder. What did betting on plain markdown cost you, and what did it buy?
3. Bases moves Obsidian into Notion's territory with plain files underneath. How far can that go — where's the real ceiling of files-as-database?
4. AI tools now read and write vaults directly. Does that strengthen the file-over-app case or eventually replace the app entirely?
5. What's the biggest thing the markdown ecosystem still gets wrong?
