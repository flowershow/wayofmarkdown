# Vision and approach

Working notes on what this site is really about. Internal for now; parts of this should graduate into the published `why.md` and section intros as they mature. (Dictated by Rufus, distilled 2026-07-18.)

## The core insight: nobody cares about markdown per se

Markdown in itself is like ASCII — you don't care about it, and "learning markdown" (the syntax) is a small, almost trivial thing. What you actually care about is what a markdown-based approach unlocks:

- **Use cases**: websites, blogs, docs, knowledgebases, wikis, catalogs — even app-like and Notion-like things (databases, views) — built from plain files.
- **The markdown-based ecosystem**: Obsidian, GitHub, Flowershow, pandoc, and the growing tool ecosystem that operates on markdown files.
- **Control and openness**: because the format is open plain text and *you* hold the files, you're never locked in. The ecosystem can reproduce the workflows that proprietary tools (Notion, wikis, CMSs) lock you into — without the lock-in.

That's why the project was once nearly called "markdown-base": the point is markdown-*based* tooling, not markdown. And it's why "The Way of Markdown" fits: markdown as a philosophy — openness, portability, ownership — expressed through practical tooling. See [[naming]].

## The two kinds of "learn"

This distinction should shape the Learn/Guides IA:

1. **Markdown basics** — the syntax core. Small, reference-flavoured, quickly done. Necessary but not the point.
2. **The markdown-based way** — the real curriculum: how to use the ecosystem to build the things you actually want. Organized by *use case* (website → blog → knowledgebase/digital garden → catalog/database → Notion-replacement), not by tool or syntax feature. The MOGF tutorials are the first arc of this.

## The showcase angle: markdown is everywhere

A third, distinct thread: illustrating just *how many* tools now speak markdown — Google Docs, Apple Notes, iPad Notes, Obsidian, GitHub, Slack, every AI chat tool. The ubiquity is itself part of the argument (network effects, convergence, safety of investing in markdown) and is cool to showcase in its own right. The kb "Markdown in Apps" notes are the seed of this; could become a "Markdown Everywhere" page/section.

## Audience

Non-technical but sophisticated people (original design doc said: initially the Life Itself team, then generic). They should be able to go from zero to a published, owned, markdown-based site — and understand why that was a good idea.

## Tone

Practical, opinionated, clear, infrastructure-minded — not grandiose. Craft ethos, not manifesto (though a Markdown Manifesto page has its place under Why). Demonstrate, don't proclaim.
