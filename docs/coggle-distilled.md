# The 2023 coggle, distilled

Source: coggle export attached to issue #12 (text + 19k-px image), mined 2026-07-18. This records what's worth reusing, what's already covered, and what goes to the backlog.

## The five reusable structures

### 1. "5 core areas people are working on" — user-intent framing ⭐

The coggle's best insight: people arrive wanting one of five things:

1. **Put a landing page on the web** (hero, call-to-action, signup)
2. **Put up a single note / post / article / report** — the humblest job: publish *one document*
3. **Create a blog** (= article + one specific "collection of stuff")
4. **Publish a collection/catalog** (portfolio, movies, books, people; can grow into linked tables)
5. **Create a knowledge garden/wiki** — interlinked docs
6. (combinations of the above)

**Gap found**: our guides cover 3–5 well, but #1 (landing page) and #2 (publish one document) have no entries — and #2 is the single simplest on-ramp we could offer. → added to content backlog (#59).

### 2. The mega-tutorial spine (validates the roadmap)

2023 version: Markdown 101 → edit a site (Part I: "GitHub direct method" → Part II: locally with Obsidian 🏆) → create/publish catalogs → collaborate (PRs). Matches today's tutorials 1–3 + catalog howto. The "GitHub direct method" vs "local method" naming is good teaching language — reuse it.

### 3. Two on-ramps: publish-first vs notes-first ⭐

A second flow sketched in the coggle: **"Start local with Obsidian vault → share on GitHub → add Flowershow to publish."** The opposite ordering from our tutorials (which publish first for the quick win). Both are valid entry motivations — "I want to publish something" vs "I want better notes (and maybe publish later)". → the roadmap page offers both entrances converging on one spine.

### 4. The question-tree skeleton — FAQ/glossary backlog

The "NEW Skeleton" section is a superb map of non-dev pain, phrased as nested questions: per tool ("What is git? Do I *need* it? How do I use it?") and per task ("What does staging mean?", "I committed something I didn't want — how do I uncommit?", "How do I fix merge conflicts?"). Plus a Key Terms glossary (repo, clone, fork, branch, push/pull, merge, PR).

→ Backlog: **kb/glossary** (git/GitHub terms for non-devs, question-phrased) and a **git-rescue howto** ("I did the wrong thing — undo it") — the unstage/uncommit/merge-conflict trio is the top fear of every newcomer. Logged in inbox #45.

### 5. Advanced catalog questions

Linked tables 🔥, private sharing 🔥, metadata types, bulk frontmatter edits, publish-later/drafts. Several are now partly answered by Obsidian Bases; they sketch the *advanced* catalog guide our current material stops short of. → future deepening of markdown-databases.

## Already covered (no action)
