# The Markdown Manifesto

> **Markdown: own the source. Compose with anything.**

In the beginning was plain text.

Plain text is the most durable digital medium we have. Files written thirty years ago open perfectly today; they will open perfectly in fifty. No platform, no vendor, no software lifecycle stands between you and your words.

**Markdown is plain text evolved** — plain text with superpowers. Headings, lists, links, tables, checklists, code: just enough structure to be powerful, while staying something you can read, write, diff, and version like any text file.

## The diagnosis

The world is drowning in proprietary formats and locked-in platforms. Your notes live in one silo, your docs in another, your website in a third — each with its own format, its own editor, its own exit barriers. The tools own the content, and therefore they own you.

## The revelation

Quietly, without a standards committee or a marketing budget, markdown has become the lingua franca of digital text. GitHub, Slack, Discord, Reddit, WhatsApp, Google Docs, Obsidian, every AI chat — everything speaks markdown now.

AI ❤️ markdown. Version control ❤️ markdown. Docs ❤️ markdown.

It started as the geek underdog in internet text areas. It's eating the world.

## The acceleration

AI makes markdown not just convenient but essential. Human–AI collaboration needs a format that is structured enough for machines, readable by humans, token-efficient, and endlessly copy-pasteable. Markdown is that format — the ideal interchange between people and machines. A folder of markdown is a knowledge base an AI can read, edit, and build on directly.

## The principle

**It's not about the format. It's about freedom — and the ecosystem.**

Nobody cares about markdown syntax, just as nobody cares which language a conversation is in — until the participants don't share one. What matters is that when your content is markdown, *every* tool can work with it: editors, publishers, databases, version control, AI. You choose the best tool for each job, combine them freely, and walk away from any of them without losing a thing.

Plain text with structure beats complex systems with brittle schemas. Constraints are a feature: markdown keeps you focused on meaning, not presentation. And where you need more, markdown extends — HTML when you want it, fenced blocks that embed diagrams, data, and whole other languages.

## The provocation

Most content tools should be rebuilt markdown-first. Notes, docs, websites, wikis, catalogs — even the database-shaped things we reach for Notion to do — can be markdown-native: plain files plus conventions plus tooling. Much of it already can be done today.

## The call

**Write in markdown. Store in markdown. Build on markdown.**

Own the source, and the whole ecosystem is yours.
