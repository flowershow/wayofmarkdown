# The Way of Markdown is live 🚀

After years of circling this (an old mind-map of mine for it just resurfaced, from 2023!), the site is live: **[The Way of Markdown](/)**, about markdown and, more importantly, everything you can build on it.

The premise, briefly: nobody actually cares about markdown. It's like caring about ASCII. What you care about is having a website, a knowledge base, your team's docs, a catalog of your books, and *not* having them locked inside somebody else's platform. Markdown plus its tool ecosystem gets you those things from plain files you own. That's the way of markdown.

What's here so far:

- **[The Markdown Roadmap](/roadmap)** 🗺️ — the whole journey as one visual, clickable map, colour-coded by what's built (it doubles as our honest to-do list)
- **[The Markdown Manifesto](/manifesto)** — the declaration, after many years of drafting
- **[Markdown-based everything](/markdown-based)** — guides for building websites, blogs, knowledge bases, a Notion replacement, databases, wikis, gardens, journals, books…
- **[Markdown Everywhere](/everywhere)** 🌍 — every app that speaks markdown, each with a how-to (and a name-and-shame corner for the holdouts)
- **[The Markdown Database Pattern](/markdown-database)** — the essay behind the most underrated trick in the ecosystem

The site is itself a folder of markdown in [a public repo](https://github.com/flowershow/wayofmarkdown), published with [Flowershow](https://flowershow.app) — we eat our own dogfood, obviously.

Poke around, and tell me what's missing or wrong: issues welcome on [GitHub](https://github.com/flowershow/wayofmarkdown/issues).
