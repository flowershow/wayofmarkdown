# Markdown-based Knowledge Bases

> [!note]
> Short version for now -- full guide coming.

A knowledge base -- personal wiki, "second brain", team brain -- is a pile of notes plus links between them. In the markdown-based version, the notes are files, the links are `[[wiki-links]]`, and the whole thing lives in a folder you own.

**The pattern**: one note per file; `[[link]]` liberally; folders and tags for loose structure; frontmatter when notes need fields. Structure emerges, you don't design it up front.

**Tools**: [Obsidian](https://obsidian.md) is the champion here -- local files, live preview, backlinks, graph view, thousands of plugins. Git/GitHub underneath gives you history and sync ([tutorial](learn/howtos/publish-obsidian-vault-to-github.md)). Publish the whole thing (or a subfolder) to the web with Flowershow when you want to share.
